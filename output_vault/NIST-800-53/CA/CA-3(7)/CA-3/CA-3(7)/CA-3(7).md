name: Information Exchange | Transitive Information Exchanges
text: "|-\n  (a) Identify transitive (downstream) information exchanges with other\
  \ systems through the systems identified in CA-3a; and\n  (b) Take measures to ensure\
  \ that transitive (downstream) information exchanges cease when the controls on\
  \ identified transitive (downstream) systems cannot be verified or validated."
discussion: Transitive or downstream information exchanges are information exchanges
  between the system or systems with which the organizational system exchanges information
  and other systems. For mission-essential systems, services, and applications, including
  high value assets, it is necessary to identify such information exchanges. The transparency
  of the controls or protection measures in place in such downstream systems connected
  directly or indirectly to organizational systems is essential to understanding the
  security and privacy risks resulting from those information exchanges. Organizational
  systems can inherit risk from downstream systems through transitive connections
  and information exchanges, which can make the organizational systems more susceptible
  to threats, hazards, and adverse impacts.
